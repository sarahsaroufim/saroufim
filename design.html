drop

Introduction

Drop is a drawing app designed to help artists warm-up, get inspired, and loosen up a little; encouraging them to benefit from the joy and therapy of drawing regularly, even if quickly or roughly. Drop offers daily curated art prompts that users can respond to by making a drop (drawing alone) or by diving into a pool (drawing with everyone). I wanted to make something that could help artists reconnect with the part of them that fell in love with drawing, before the personal, financial and social pressures.

Branding

  • Name

    The Drop app is like water: quick and refreshing like a splash, ever-flowing and constantly renewing like a stream, calming and reflective like a pond. A drop is a small dose – I want people to draw a little every day. Finally, Drop sounds like Draw-p :)

  • Logo

    The logo visually combines the two main features of the app: the solo experience as represented by the drop, and the collective experience as represented by the pool. Together they resemble the tip of a pencil.

  • Colors

    The brand colors are the same colors you draw with. This palette is restrictive and unnatural – it reminds users that this is a loose, creative and non-serious space and encourages them to draw with a casual mindset.

    Your icon color changes depending on your chosen in-app color theme.

  • Pool widget

    Rather than threats of losing your streak, the Drop widget is a live window into the Pool activity. It hints at the theme of the day and piques your curiosity, inviting you into the app organically.

tutor orial

Introduction

Tutor Orial is an online tutor booking service for college students that was experiencing low completion rates on their website, as well as complaints about the booking process. I examined the service's sign-up flow to identify users' pain points with it, and to better re-imagine Tutor Orial's brand, mission and interface. The new and improved Tutor Orial embodies the idea that learning can and should be fun, with various interactions and an experience that challenges the conventions of typical academic platforms.

Scope

Heuristic analysis, UX research, User testing, Interaction design, Illustration, Brand identity, Re-design

smash gym

Introduction

Smash is a gym in Mansourieh, Lebanon that was looking to redefine their visual identity to better reflect their brand values and attract new customers. They were also looking to establish a social media presence for the business. Best of all, they wanted to makeover the interior of the fitness center, which we decked out with fresh paint and sick vinyl wall graphics.

dammeh

Introduction

Dammeh (meaning Hug or Embrace in Arabic) is a collection of eleven profoundly moving and at times dark short stories written by renowned Lebanese author Zeinab Charafeddine. The stories find human beings wrestling with life's great passages and challenges, and play with the boundaries between imagination, memory and realism. Big hug to Snoubar Bayrout and to Zeinab for prioritizing a sensitive approach to creating this book and for trusting me with interpreting her vulnerable writings into an object to complement their beauty and thoughtfulness.

Scope

Publication design, Typesetting, Lettering, Illustration, Print production

Timeline

Jun 2022 - Dec 2022
